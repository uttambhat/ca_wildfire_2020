<p>The <b>2018 California wildfires</b> are a series of <a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfires</a> that have burned across the state of <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> during the year of 2018. A national disaster was declared in Northern California on August 4, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> A total of 5,168 fires had burned an area of 895,386 acres (3,623.50&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), according to the <a href="/wiki/California_Department_of_Forestry_and_Fire_Protection" title="California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection">California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Interagency_Fire_Center" title="National Interagency Fire Center">National Interagency Fire Center</a>, as of August 12.<sup id="cite_ref-CalFire_Stats_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CalFire_Stats-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SACC_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SACC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SACC_website_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SACC_website-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The active <a href="/wiki/Mendocino_Complex_Fire" title="Mendocino Complex Fire">Mendocino Complex Fire</a> has burned more than 344,000 acres (1,390&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), becoming the largest complex fire in the state's history, with the complex's Ranch Fire surpassing the <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Fire" title="Thomas Fire">Thomas Fire</a> to become California's single-largest modern wildfire.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ranch_Fire_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ranch_Fire-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Increased_fire_susceptibility">Increased fire susceptibility</span></h2>
<p>Many different factors led to the 2018 California wildfire season becoming so devastating. A combination of an increased amount of natural fuel and compounding atmospheric conditions from <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" class="mw-redirect" title="Global warming">global warming</a> led to a series of destructive fires.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Increase_in_fuel">Increase in fuel</span></h3>
<p>A direct contributor to the 2018 California wildfires was an increase in dead tree fuel.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> By December 2017, there were a record 129 million dead trees in California.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atmospheric_conditions">Atmospheric conditions</span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford</a> <a href="/wiki/Earth_System_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Earth System Science">Earth System Science</a> Professor <a href="/wiki/Noah_Diffenbaugh" title="Noah Diffenbaugh">Noah Diffenbaugh</a> stated that atmospheric conditions for <a href="/wiki/California_wildfires" class="mw-redirect" title="California wildfires">California wildfires</a> are expected to worsen in the future because of the effects of <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_in_California" title="Climate change in California">climate change in California</a> and that "what we're seeing over the last few years in terms of the wildfire season in California [is] very consistent with the historical trends in terms of increasing temperatures, increasing dryness, and increasing wildfire risk". Other experts agreed, saying that <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" class="mw-redirect" title="Global warming">global warming</a> is to blame for these extreme weather conditions. <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" class="mw-redirect" title="Global warming">Global warming</a> resulted in higher temperatures and less rain, creating a drier landscape that gave fires more fuel to burn longer and stronger.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_construction_in_the_wildland-urban_interface">Home construction in the wildland-urban interface</span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Wildland%E2%80%93urban_interface" title="Wildland–urban interface">wildland–urban interface</a> (or WUI) refers to the zone of transition between unoccupied land and human development. Communities that are within 0.5 miles (0.80 km) of the zone may also be included. These lands and communities adjacent to and surrounded by wildlands are at risk of wildfires.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Since the 1990s, over 43% of new homes have been constructed in this area. In some areas it's nearly 80%.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In the past, when these areas burned, no homes were lost, but now homes are there and are being destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Air_quality">Air quality</span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fatalities">Fatalities</span></h2>
<p>On June 4, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Panoche_Fire&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Panoche Fire (page does not exist)">Panoche Fire</a> broke out, in a series of three blazes that started in the <a href="/wiki/San_Benito_County,_California" title="San Benito County, California">San Benito County</a> area. While the Panoche incident was the smallest of the three fires, burning only 64 acres (26&#160;ha), the remains of three people were found in a destroyed camping trailer in the burn area.<sup id="cite_ref-found_dead_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-found_dead-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> The remains were believed to belong to a mother, a toddler, and an infant.<sup id="cite_ref-found_dead_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-found_dead-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 14, a <a href="/wiki/Cal_Fire" class="mw-redirect" title="Cal Fire">Cal Fire</a> bulldozer operator was killed while fighting the <a href="/wiki/Ferguson_Fire" title="Ferguson Fire">Ferguson Fire</a> becoming the first firefighter death of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-FergusonDeath_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FergusonDeath-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 23, the <a href="/wiki/Carr_Fire" title="Carr Fire">Carr Fire</a> broke out after a vehicle malfunctioned. While the <a href="/wiki/Carr_Fire" title="Carr Fire">Carr Fire</a> burned in rural areas of <a href="/wiki/Shasta_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Shasta County">Shasta County</a> for the first few days, it crossed the <a href="/wiki/Sacramento_River" title="Sacramento River">Sacramento River</a> and entered the city limits of <a href="/wiki/Redding,_California" title="Redding, California">Redding, California</a> on the evening of July 26. By the next morning, 2 firefighters and 4 civilians would be dead.<sup id="cite_ref-CarrDeath_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CarrDeath-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CarrFirefighterDeath_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CarrFirefighterDeath-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CarrFire6Deaths_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CarrFire6Deaths-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>As of the afternoon of July 29, there are 7 people still unaccounted for.<sup id="cite_ref-CarrMissing_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CarrMissing-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 29, a firefighter with the <a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">National Park Service</a> was killed after a dead tree fell and struck him, while he was fighting the <a href="/wiki/Ferguson_Fire" title="Ferguson Fire">Ferguson Fire</a>. He was "treated on scene, but died before he could be taken to the hospital".<sup id="cite_ref-SecondDeath_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SecondDeath-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 4, a <a href="/wiki/PG%26E" class="mw-redirect" title="PG&amp;E">PG&amp;E</a> employee was killed in a vehicle incident while working to restore services to areas impacted by the Carr Fire.<sup id="cite_ref-PG&amp;E_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PG&amp;E-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 9, a CAL Fire heavy equipment mechanic was killed in a traffic incident while working at the Carr Fire.<sup id="cite_ref-Eighth_Carr_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eighth_Carr-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
